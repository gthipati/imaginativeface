    Magpies | 2015

    This is the final project for my Worldbuilding class. Each student individually researched and developed an original graphic novel concept that addresses a relevant, societal issue and is presented in the form of a mock Wikipedia page. My story, Magpies, is inspired by the internal racism and environmental state of modern day China. It also borrows plotlines from the Chinese myth of “The Weaver Girl and the Cowherd”. I began with researching China’s factory work environment, as well as their policies in regards to the infamous pollution in the more industrial parts of the country. We were also required to select a myth to use as an inspiration or the basis of our plotline. I decided to choose the Chinese myth that is the origin story of the Qixi Festival. From it, I borrowed the use of setting up lights as a celebration of love, union, and hope.

    A requirement of this project, and a core value of the class, was to an unlikely protagonist that isn’t heroic or conveniently more special than the other characters but is, as Wayne puts it, someone “from below and to the left”. I struggled to understand this concept as I am used to protagonists who are strong because they, by chance, had special ability that made them strong. Think Superman, who was born with super-strength, and thus blessed with beautiful women (Lois Lane), and power. How do I make an interesting character that is realistic and relatable, but interesting at the same time? It helped to read some dystopian novels like Parable of the Sower by Octavia E. Butler and Oryx and Crake by Margaret Atwood where the protagonist is just as messed up as their peers and their world. They have no special power and but through luck (or misfortune) of their circumstance they find strength to push farther and create an important and interesting plot.

    Li, the protagonist of Magpies, is the young girl living in a fictional poor, factory town. The sky is constantly gray with smog, and the citizens are just as lifeless and gray. Li has big dreams to see a starlit sky and is inspired by her grandparents’ stories about the lantern-lit festivals full of joy and life to revive the forgotten festival culture in her town. She hopes that the revival of a festival that the town has forgotten will re-inspire the townspeople to look up instead of down and to be able to hope for a better future.

    We did several “mini comics” throughout the course that depict scenes that would be in our graphic novel if we actual wrote it out.

    At the end of the project, each section voted for one graphic novel concept to produce for our class’s final mock Comic Con. My story, though well received by the class, was not chosen. I did end up doing the artwork for the graphic novel we did feature, though.
