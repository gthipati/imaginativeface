    Sound Project | 2016


    For my Media 101 class, our group of 3 recorded and edited a two-minute layered sound piece about a suspenseful chase between the main character and a stalker. This project has no video and only uses original sounds produced by us. The point of this project was to practice creating sounds, including ambient, by using every day items.

    Concept

    The assignment required us to put together sounds to tell a story of a journey of our choice. The aspects of this assignment include brainstorming, storyboarding, capturing sound with various types of recording equipment, and editing using Adobe Premiere. My role in this project was mainly pre-production (storyboarding) and post-production (editing).

    Since our first video Project was mystery themed, we decided to continue with darker themes in this project. After we decided that the story would include only two characters: the stalker and the victim, we brainstormed an outline for the story. Our first problem was deciding how the listener would distinguish the two characters since there wasn’t going to be any dialogue. The most logical option was to go with the sound of the characters’ footsteps, which depending on the shoes they wore.

    Our initial decision was to have the victim be a woman wearing some type of heeled shoe while the stalker was a male wearing sneakers. After discussing it with our TA, we decided to that it would be more interesting if we flipped the genders from what most people would expect in a stalker/victim scenario. Therefore, the victim became a younger male and the stalker was a woman wearing thick heeled shoes.

    On-Site Recording

    Since majority of the piece needed to be filmed outdoors to gather accurate sounds of footsteps on various terrains (concrete, steel stairs) and the sound of still outdoor air, we chose our shooting location based on how populated the filming area would be. We needed a lot of specific sounds and couldn’t have the interference of random, unneeded noises. That said, we still encountered a few instances where it was still too difficult to gather a specific sound. For example, we wanted the buzz off a street light but the constant airplane engines and strong wind prevented us from getting the sound we wanted. Eventually, we used a generator that gave off a similar hum and lowered the intensity of the sound when we got to the editing room.

    Editing

    We used Adobe Premiere to edit our project as that was the program being taught. My job was to quality check our sounds files, which was important because we wanted minimal outside noise in each individual sound. After, I would do a rough cut where I layered the sounds and sequenced them approximately to match our storyboard. That way, my group could determine whether we needed to record additional sounds to make the story sound more realistic or re-record corrupted sounds.

    The Zone | 2017

    I currently work as a graphic designer for The Zone at UC San Diego. The Zone is a wellness lounge for students and hosts various free programs promoting a healthy life and well-being.

    Designing for The Zone requires us to be mindful about how our designs market the space. We want to promote an open, inclusive space where students can relax, lift their mood, and learn ways to lead a healthier lifestyle. Some of my work this quarter includes re-designing two, 11inx17in posters that market specific programs that The Zone offers.

    Body Composition

    Students can come in for a free analysis of their body weight, blood pressure, water content, and body fat percentage. We didn’t want the poster to include images of weights, scales, and body types as those images could be triggering to certain individuals. The Zone also seeks to promote overall health, not specific body types or specific definitions of what “health” means. This is also the first piece I’ve ever designed completely on Adobe Illustrator.

    Meditation

    This program encourages students to take a step back from their hectic college life and focus on their breath. I wanted the poster convey a sense of peacefulness and calm. The text about the program was created in collaboration with the instructor that leads the weekly meditation sessions.

    Weekly Calendar

    Another one is updating the weekly calendar that is posted on our website and sent our in e-mail newsletters. Throughout our 10-week quarter as well as finals week, the calendar lists the time and name of programs that are offered this week. I was given creative freedom with the design of the calendar.
